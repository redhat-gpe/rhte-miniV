OCP Sustainability Lab - Kepler

Experience using Kepler to measure container energy consumption.

Kepler is a software tool that enables users to measure the energy consumption of containers. It is built on top of Kubernetes and uses eBPF (extended Berkeley Packet Filter) technology to probe energy-related system statistics. eBPF is a tracing and profiling technology that has been integrated into the Linux kernel, enabling Kepler to access low-level kernel information and collect data on the energy consumption of containerized applications. Kepler exports this data as Prometheus metrics, allowing organizations to analyze energy consumption trends over time.

Kepler is designed to be lightweight and user-friendly, with a simple interface that streamlines the process of collecting energy consumption data. Its integration with Kubernetes also makes it highly scalable, making it suitable for large-scale container deployments. By providing insights into container energy usage, Kepler can help organizations optimize their energy consumption and reduce their carbon footprint.
